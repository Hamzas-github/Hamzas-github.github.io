# About me

I'm **Muhammad Hamza Farooq**, a **Data Analyst** who likes the unglamorous part of
the job: taking data that's messy, incomplete, or mislabelled and turning it into
something a decision-maker can trust.

My work tends to follow the same arc — **clean it, model it, query it, visualize it,
and explain it** — because an analysis is only useful if the person reading it
understands what it means and where it can be trusted.

## What I work with

- **Languages & tools:** Python (pandas, NumPy), SQL, SQLite
- **Visualization & BI:** Matplotlib, Seaborn, Power BI
- **Practices:** reproducible pipelines, defensive data cleaning, clear written
  reporting, and honest data caveats

## How I approach a problem

1. **Start from the question**, not the dataset — what decision does this support?
2. **Clean defensively** — validate, handle outliers, and document every choice.
3. **Engineer the features** the question actually needs (e.g. deriving geography
   from free-text addresses).
4. **Answer in SQL**, then **visualize** so the finding is obvious at a glance.
5. **Write it up** — findings, recommendations, and the limits of the data.

You can see all of this in practice in my
**[London Rental Market Analysis case study](/projects/london-rental-analysis)**.
